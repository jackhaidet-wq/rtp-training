CHEAT SHEET 14 - MULTI-STEP AGENT PATTERNS
Track 3 - Cowork Deep Dive / Lesson 3.3
================================================================

THE PATTERN
  ORIENT  ->  PLAN  ->  ACT  ->  CHECK  ->  REPORT

ORIENT
  Claude reads the inputs you've given it and figures out
  what it actually has and what it's missing.
    Example: "Read everything in the working folder.
              Tell me what you have, what you're missing,
              and what you'd ask before starting."

PLAN
  Claude proposes the steps before doing any of them.
    Example: "Write the plan as a numbered list. Don't
              execute yet. Tell me which steps you're
              uncertain about."

ACT
  Claude executes one step at a time, with a tool call
  per step, with you approving connector calls per use.

CHECK
  After each step (or at the end), Claude reviews its own
  work for errors, missing inputs, or unverified numbers.
    Example: "Re-read your output. Flag anything that
              depends on a number you didn't cite or a
              fact you couldn't verify."

REPORT
  Claude returns a summary of what it did, what it found,
  what it could NOT do, and what it skipped.

----------------------------------------------------------------
WORKED EXAMPLE - CASCADE HOLDINGS NEWS MONITOR
----------------------------------------------------------------
  PROMPT:
    "You are running a multi-step research task on Cascade
     Holdings. Workflow:
       1. Orient: list what's in the working folder.
       2. Plan: outline a 5-step research workflow to
          produce a 1-page sector-news brief on Cascade.
       3. Act: execute step by step. Stop after each step
          and tell me what you did and what's next.
       4. Check: at the end, re-read and flag anything
          unverified.
       5. Report: produce the 1-page brief with citations."

----------------------------------------------------------------
WHERE MULTI-STEP BREAKS - BE HONEST ABOUT THIS
----------------------------------------------------------------
  - LONG-RUNNING COST. Many steps = many tool calls = real
    spend. The cost cap exists for a reason.
  - RECOVERY FROM A BAD STEP. If step 3 produced nonsense,
    steps 4 and 5 are built on sand. CHECK at every step.
  - HALLUCINATED TOOL CALLS. Rare, but Claude can claim it
    called a tool it didn't. The admin tool-call log is
    the source of truth.
  - DRIFT. The longer the chain, the more the original
    intent gets watered down. Keep prompts tight.

----------------------------------------------------------------
RULES OF THUMB
----------------------------------------------------------------
  - 3-5 steps is the sweet spot for most workflows.
  - >7 steps -> consider breaking into a Skill or Scheduled
    Task.
  - Always insert a CHECK before any irreversible step
    (send, post, write to a system).
