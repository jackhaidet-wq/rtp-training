================================================================
CHEAT SHEET 08 - WHAT STAYS OUT OF CLAUDE
Track 2 - Governance / Lesson 2.3 (THE BRIGHT LINE)
================================================================

FOUR THINGS NEVER GO IN CLAUDE

  1. MATERIAL NON-PUBLIC INFORMATION (MNPI)
     Anything you wouldn't email to a competitor.
     Earnings before release. M&A discussions before
     announcement.

  2. DEAL TERMS
     LOIs, term sheets, definitive agreement language for
     any active deal.

  3. LP DATA
     Names, allocations, capital call schedules, side
     letters - anything tied to a specific LP.

  4. PORTFOLIO COMPANY FINANCIALS
     Revenue, EBITDA, headcount detail, customer lists for
     any portfolio company.

If you're unsure: ASK WILL OR HENRY before you paste.
There is no penalty for asking.
There is a real penalty for guessing wrong.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SPOT-THE-VIOLATION - 3 SCENARIOS
----------------------------------------------------------------

SCENARIO 1
  You're prepping a deal screen and want Claude to compare
  your target's revenue trajectory to a portfolio company
  you already own. You paste the portco's last four quarters
  of revenue (a public-ish figure from a press release) into
  Cowork.

  ANSWER: NOT OK.
  Even revenue figures from a press release become PORTCO
  FINANCIALS the moment they're combined with your firm's
  analysis. Use Verity for portco research and keep raw
  financials out of the working folder.

SCENARIO 2
  An MD forwards you an unsigned LOI from a sell-side banker
  with the line "help me think through the price." You drop
  the PDF into your Cowork working folder.

  ANSWER: NOT OK.
  An LOI - signed or not - is a deal term. Bright line.
  Summarize the open price questions yourself first; ask
  Claude to react to your summary; the LOI never touches
  the working folder.

SCENARIO 3
  You're researching a target. You drop the target's public
  S-1 into Cowork and ask Claude to summarize the customer-
  concentration risk.

  ANSWER: OK.
  The S-1 is publicly filed. No MNPI, no deal terms, no LP
  data, no portco financials. This is exactly what Cowork
  is for.

----------------------------------------------------------------
THE ONE-QUESTION TEST BEFORE YOU PASTE
----------------------------------------------------------------
  Does this content - on its own or combined with what's
  already in the chat - reveal MNPI, a deal term, an LP, or
  a portco financial?

  If yes, or if you can't tell -> DON'T paste. Ask first.
