CHEAT SHEET 01 - HOW LLMs ACTUALLY WORK
Track 1 - AI 101 / Lesson 1.1
================================================================

THE 30-SECOND VERSION
  An LLM is a next-token predictor trained on a snapshot of
  public text. It is NOT a search engine and it does NOT know
  anything new unless you give it tools (web, files, connectors).

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU
  - Claude doesn't "look things up" by default.
  - Claude can confabulate - confidently produce false specifics.
  - Citations Claude produces are pointers, not proof. Verify.
  - Knowledge cutoff matters. Anything recent needs web/connector.

THE RESURGENS POSTURE
  - Anthropic 30-day retention default.
  - User Feedback OFF org-wide.
  - Your prompts are NOT used to train models.

THE HONEST ANALOGY
  Claude is the best junior analyst you've ever hired who has
  never been to your office and reads everything you give it.
  Treat it accordingly.

WHAT TO DO ON EVERY ANSWER
  1. Ask: did Claude have the data it needed, or did it guess?
  2. Cross-check any number Claude produced.
  3. If a citation matters, click through and confirm.
  4. If you're going to act on it, name your assumptions.

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
  - What did Claude NOT know about the firm before you told it?
  - Where could a confabulation cost the firm money?
